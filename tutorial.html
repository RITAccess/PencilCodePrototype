Accessible PencilCode Foundation

Tutorial

Welcome to the Accessible Pencil Code simulation. This tutorial will walk you through this simulation's auditory cues, as well as the procedure for tasks you will be asked to complete.

This simulation will use three different forms of auditory cues. You will hear them being used in different orders, but all together at a time. The first is speech. This simulation will use regular speech to indicate Pencil Code functions.

The second form of auditory cue is earcons. These are synthesized musical sounds. We will teach you how to use this simulation's earcons shortly.

The third and final form is spearcons. These are sped-up speech. They should be as self-explanatory as speech. Let us know later if you have issues understanding any of the three audible cues.

We will now conduct earcon training. There will be buttons for you to click. Listen to what the earcons stand for, then press the buttons to hear the actual earcons.
